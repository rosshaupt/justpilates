Pilates

Josef Pilates was born in Dusseldorf in 1880 – a frail sickly child who suffered from rickets, asthma and rheumatic fever. Determined to overcome these weaknesses, he experimented with many different approaches to body work, martial arts, gymnastics, dance and weight training to name but a few. In selecting the most effective features of these disciplines, he devised a method to provide the perfect balance between strength and flexibility.

Pilates is the ultimate mind-body workout. A fitness phenomenon of recent years, it has gained widespread support from the worlds of sport and medicine. Pilates is a body-conditioning method that works in a different way to other fitness techniques. Targeting the deep postural muscles, it works by building strength from the inside out, rebalancing the body and bringing it into correct postural alignment. This method of stretching and strengthening in a precise and natural rhythm produces profound results for people of all ages and abilities.

The benefits are life changing…

It helps to reshape your body, which will become longer, leaner and more toned.

It will improve your posture, achieving the perfect balance between strength and flexibility.

It relieves unwanted stress and tension.

It creates a stronger more flexible spine.

It improves lung capacity and circulation.

It increases mobility, agility and stamina.

It focuses the mind and enhances body awareness.

It is specially recommended by medical specialists for those with back problems.

 

If you are new to Pilates it is essential that you enrol for 3 private lessons. During this time you will understand the principles of stabilizing your body through the use of your ‘core’ muscles. Justine will take you through a range of simple movements which effectively target these stabilizing muscles and ensure that you understand what you are doing. By the end of the third session you should feel confident about finding your ‘core’ muscles, able to activate and strengthen them and know how to align your body to achieve the best results.

At this point you will be able to decide if you would like to continue with private lessons or whether classes are more suitable.

"Physical fitness is the first requisite of happiness. In order to achieve happiness, it is imperative to gain mastery of your body. If at the age of 30 you are stiff and out of shape, you are old. If at 60 you are supple and strong then you are young." Joseph Pilates
